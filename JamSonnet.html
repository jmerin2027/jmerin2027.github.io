A Longing for Time

Shakespearean

The little laughs now echo through the records of my mind
Ignoring a deadline so distant drained all the doubt
Now that day has long passed, leaving longing behind

After every parting, we would see each other again
Yet I wish I could run back to the old days
Never feared farewell, we knew the next meeting back then
After a greeting, Never want you to leave my gaze

So I'll close my eyes and wander through the winds
Staring at the horizon present during days of old
I started feeling content with digging pictures in bins
Until greed struck and yearned for more than gold

But the trust we share is special, time travel is too cheap
I’ll endure until then and be content with reminiscing in my sleep
