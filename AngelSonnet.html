Embracing Grace in Paradise: Rekindling Love's Light in Alonica

Petrarchan

In LA, my love for you begins to wane,
Reasons to stay enthralled elude my grasp.
The chill in the air whispers a parting gasp,
Melancholy, I feel I now refrain.

In Alonica, where sunshine remains,
A cherished beach where sands stretch and clasp,
As my heart seeks solace, I recap the past.
Alonica, my home, is a retreat worries now restrain.

In this paradise, my being is now rooted deep.
I sing and dance as the morning sun beguiles,
The stars, my old friends, I seek.

A place where I feel most at home, complete,
With favorite beach, stretching for endless miles,
My heart learns the art, to love again, replete.

Eternally Mourning: The Painful Echoes of a Love that Was

Shakespearean

The greatest romance, lighting strike to my heart,
In the headlights, our slow dance took flight,
No chance of ever falling apart,
But now I’m here, yet distant from your sight.

Running from the sun, seeking a hiding place,
Nowhere left to hide, nowhere to run free,
Every night I close my eyes to embrace
A love I wish was still, but cannot be.

At war with my mind, fighting daily,
To let you go, it’s a painful endeavor,
This hurt will linger, it won’t go greatly,
But I won’t regret loving you, no, never.

For even though someone will love you incredibly,
I loved you first, and that will last endlessly.
